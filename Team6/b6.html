Eastern Extravaganza

Sovani's 'Dhokla Ya Pakoda', an interesting mix of fried and steamed gram flour tinged with the gorgeous flavour of tamarind and mint, was a socio-cultural leveller, fuelling debates on whether it was Gujarat or Uttar Pradesh that could lay claim to this rotund creature. And in his own words, it is the win of the tastebuds in the end.

Cultural convergence finds strong resonance across this duet act. Singh, who has spent his formative years in Asansol in a Communist-ruled West Bengal, says that the place has had tremendous influence on his cooking. In his signature Carpaccio of Scottish Salmon, a perfectly-cured salmon is served with a green pea mustard chutney and topped with jhaal-muri or puffed rice, a made-in-Bengal specialty. And it's not only just the jhaal-muri but also in the Surf-n-turf scallop and lamb galouti that the personal(memory) finds representation.

Cultural appropriation continues in Singh's homage to duck (he is fascinated by cooking with game meat) with a winner in the duck and beetroot spring roll. And Sovani continues the thread - by cooking up a 630-poached egg which is a generous concoction of Goan pork churris, caramelized onion and cashew sauce.

The British when they went back from India, took back curry and spice. People in the West are now discovering the benefits of spice that can keep one, warm, happy, healed and de-toxed.
